Song Twisting Guide

How to weave multiple songs as a bard on Quarm

Overview

Song twisting is the core mechanic that separates a good bard from a great one. By timing your song casts correctly, you can maintain the effects of multiple songs simultaneously, even though only one can be actively playing at a time.

Each song has a 12-second duration once cast. By cycling through songs before each expires, you keep all their effects active on your group. The number of songs you can twist depends on cast time and your reaction speed.

Twist Timing by Song Count

2-Song Twist

Each song gets ~9s of effect time. Easiest to maintain. Good for leveling and when you need to do other things between casts.

3-Song Twist

Standard raid twist. ~6s effective time per song. Requires steady attention but very manageable with practice.

4-Song Twist

Expert level. Tight timing with short gaps. Some songs may drop briefly between cycles. Only attempt when comfortable with 3-song.

Advanced Techniques

Melody Command Macros

▶

The /melody command automates song twisting. Use it to queue up songs in order:

/melody 1 2 3 — twists songs in slots 1, 2, and 3 automatically.

You can interrupt melody at any time to handle charm breaks, pulls, or manual song casts. Re-run the /melody command to restart the cycle.

Twisting During Combat

▶

During combat, prioritize keeping your haste and ATK songs up. If you need to drop a song, drop the lowest priority one from your chain. Resist songs can be woven in for specific encounters.

Practice Checklist

Practice 2-song twist for 10 minutes
▶

Start with Selo's and any other song. Focus on the rhythm: cast song 1, wait for cast to finish, cast song 2, repeat. Get comfortable with the timing before adding more songs.

Upgrade to 3-song twist
▶

Add a third song. The timing is tighter — you'll notice songs starting to drop if you're too slow. Use /melody 1 2 3 to automate if available.

Set up /melody macros for common rotations
▶

Create hotkeys for your most-used rotations: melee group, caster group, solo kiting. Having these ready means you can switch roles instantly.
