Overview

Ssraeshza Temple is the home of the Shissar -- ancient serpent-like creatures banished to Luclin millennia ago. The temple has multiple floors, with the deeper areas housing increasingly powerful enemies culminating in Emperor Ssraeshza (level 66, 1,250,000 HP) at the top. The temple is accessed from The Deep on Luclin.

The lower floors of the temple are accessible to anyone. However, there are two separate keys required to fully access the temple's upper areas:

  • Zazuzh's Key (item 19711) -- drops from Commander Zazuzh; opens a locked door on the mid-level floors.
  • Ring of the Shissar (item 19719) -- crafted by combining three No Drop components in the Taskmaster's Pouch; activates the teleporter to Emperor Ssraeshza's chamber. Also a wearable finger slot ring: +8 AC, +4 INT, +4 STA, +4 WIS, +80 HP, +65 Mana. Magic, Lore, No Drop, All/All.

The quest information comes from Elder Glanoxx, an Iksar slave held captive within the temple itself. When you hail him, he looks sick and motions for something to drink. Give him a Water Flask and follow the dialogue keywords to learn the secret of the key.

Phase 1 — Elder Glanoxx & the Secret of the Key

Ssraeshza Temple (Lower Floors) — NPC: Elder Glanoxx

▶
Locate Elder Glanoxx and hail him
▶

Elder Glanoxx is an Iksar slave held captive in the lower floors of Ssraeshza Temple. When you hail him:

Elder Glanoxx looks like he's been sick for some time. He looks at you for a moment and motions for something to drink.
Give Elder Glanoxx a Water Flask
▶

Hand Elder Glanoxx a Water Flask (item 13006 -- a common drink sold by many vendors). He responds:

'Are you here for them... are you here to release us?'
Follow the dialogue keywords to learn the secret
▶

Say "release". Glanoxx responds:

'The ones that keep us here... the ones who defy the gods. They must be destroyed. I cannot recall a time outside of this place, but I believe the stories. I trust that we are from somewhere else. These creatures claim to be gods. They claim to have created us from the filth of the mines. This cannot be. I have seen the face of our true god. He told me of their blasphemy. He came to me and handed me the knowledge of how to reach their Emperor. He taught me to resist the prying magic of the fanatics. Are you the crusaders of prophecy... the ones who walk in the mist?'

Say "crusaders of prophecy". He responds:

'The prophecy will come to pass at last. Take my knowledge... the knowledge of our lord. I will not last much longer. This sickness will kill me like the others. There is one other who can help you. He claims to know some weakness of these creatures, seek him at the smelting furnaces near the mines. Now I must tell you the secret of the key.'

Say "secret of the key". Glanoxx reveals the three items you need:

'There is a pouch to be found. It was specially made and requires three separate components to function. Seek out the Idol of Zazuzh and the Ring of Zeruzsh. These items are kept by the Emperor's must loyal Taskmasters. Place with them the Ssreashzian Insignia. This item will be much harder to acquire. I am sure you will be successful... our lord walks with you. Please be swift.'

Glanoxx uses slightly different names than the actual item names:

  • "Idol of Zazuzh" = Zazuzh's Idol -- drops from Commander Zazuzh, not the Taskmasters
  • "Ring of Zeruzsh" = Zeruzsh's Ring -- drops from Commander Zherozsh, not the Taskmasters
  • "Ssreashzian Insignia" = Ssraeshzian Insignia -- drops from General Kizuhx, Advisor Zekuzh, or Arbiter Korazhk

The Taskmaster's Pouch he mentions drops from the named Taskmasters, Warden Mekuzh, and Rhozth Ssravizh throughout the temple.

Phase 2 — Killing Commanders & Collecting Components

Ssraeshza Temple — Component Drops from Named NPCs

▶
Obtain a Taskmaster's Pouch from a named Taskmaster, Warden, or Rhozth
▶

The Taskmaster's Pouch (item 17118) is a 4-slot No Drop container required for the final combine. It is consumed when you combine. It drops from the following NPCs, all level 60 with 32,000 HP:

  • Taskmaster Keuzozh (NPC 162021)
  • Taskmaster Zhe`Vozh (NPC 162059)
  • Taskmaster Zerumaz (NPC 162011)
  • Taskmaster Kavamezh (NPC 162012)
  • Taskmaster Vezhkah (NPC 162024)
  • Taskmaster Mikazha (NPC 162013)
  • Taskmaster Revan`Kezh (NPC 162060)
  • Warden Mekuzh (NPC 162023)
  • Rhozth Ssravizh (NPC 162089)

This is usually the easiest piece to obtain since there are nine potential sources. Farm this first while organizing the harder Commander/General kills.

Kill Commander Zazuzh for Zazuzh's Idol and Zazuzh's Key
▶

Commander Zazuzh (NPC 162003, level 56, 17,500 HP) drops two important items:

  • Zazuzh's Idol (item 19716) -- one of the three combine components (100% drop rate, No Drop)
  • Zazuzh's Key (item 19711) -- No Drop key that opens the locked door on the mid-level floors

Commander Zazuzh is a challenging named Shissar requiring a well-geared group. He has max hit of 150 and a 3,500 HP enrage threshold.

Kill Commander Zherozsh for Zeruzsh's Ring
▶

Commander Zherozsh (NPC 162027, level 58, 18,270 HP) drops Zeruzsh's Ring (item 19717) at a 100% drop rate (No Drop). He is the higher-level of the two Commanders with a max hit of 150 and a 3,500 HP enrage threshold. Bring a solid group with crowd control.

Kill General Kizuhx, Advisor Zekuzh, or Arbiter Korazhk for the Ssraeshzian Insignia
▶

The Ssraeshzian Insignia (item 19718) is the third and hardest combine component. It drops (No Drop, 100% drop rate) from three different named NPCs, all level 53 with 150,000 HP -- despite their lower level, these are among the most powerful non-boss mobs in the temple:

  • General Kizuhx (NPC 162066) -- max hit 4,500
  • Advisor Zekuzh (NPC 162017) -- max hit 4,500, casts spells
  • Arbiter Korazhk (NPC 162018) -- max hit 4,500

Elder Glanoxx warns that this item will be "much harder to acquire" -- and he's right. With 150,000 HP and 4,500 max hit, these NPCs are significantly tougher than the Commanders and require a strong raid force to take down. For comparison, the Commanders have only ~18,000 HP.

Phase 3 — The Taskmaster's Pouch Combine

Combine the three components in the Taskmaster's Pouch

▶
Place all three components into the Taskmaster's Pouch and Combine
▶

Once you have all three items, place them into the Taskmaster's Pouch:

  1. Zazuzh's Idol (item 19716)
  2. Zeruzsh's Ring (item 19717)
  3. Ssraeshzian Insignia (item 19718)

Hit Combine. This is a no-fail combine (recipe 9933) that does not require any tradeskill level. The pouch is consumed in the process (replace_container). The result is the Ring of the Shissar.

Receive the Ring of the Shissar
▶

The Ring of the Shissar (item 19719) is both a wearable ring and a key item:

  • Slot: Finger
  • AC: 8
  • Stats: +4 INT, +4 STA, +4 WIS
  • HP/Mana: +80 HP, +65 Mana
  • Flags: MAGIC LORE NO DROP, All Classes / All Races
Use Zazuzh's Key and the Ring of the Shissar to access the upper temple
▶

There are two key-locked barriers in the temple (verified from the door table):

  • Zazuzh's Key (item 19711) -- opens the locked door at the mid-level floors (door ID 8924, near coordinates 221, 528, 129). Keep this in your inventory to pass through.
  • Ring of the Shissar (item 19719) -- activates the fungus translocator (door ID 8926, at coordinates -881, 235, 279) that teleports you to the Emperor's chamber area (coordinates 620, -324, 405). Keep this in your inventory to use the teleporter.

With both keys, you have full access to the entire temple, including Emperor Ssraeshza's room and the bosses leading to him.

Tips & Notes

  • All key items are No Drop. The Ring of the Shissar, Zazuzh's Key, Zazuzh's Idol, Zeruzsh's Ring, the Ssraeshzian Insignia, and the Taskmaster's Pouch are all No Drop. Every player who needs the ring must loot their own components.
  • The Taskmaster's Pouch is the easiest piece to get since it drops from nine different NPCs (seven Taskmasters plus Warden Mekuzh and Rhozth Ssravizh, all level 60 with 32,000 HP). Farm this first while organizing the harder kills. Note: the pouch is consumed on combine.
  • The three Insignia mobs (General Kizuhx, Advisor Zekuzh, Arbiter Korazhk) each have 150,000 HP and hit for up to 4,500 -- far more than the Commanders (~18,000 HP, ~150 max hit). These are raid-level encounters.
  • The Iksar foreman near the smelting furnaces (referenced by Elder Glanoxx) has optional dialogue about tainted green metal from the mines that Shissar despise. Say "glanoxx sent me", then "weakness", then "tainted metal". He reveals you can taint Shissar weapons with the green metal to exploit their weakness.

Emperor Ssraeshza Encounter Details

  • Xerkizh The Creator (NPC 162190, level 66, 800,000 HP, max hit 24,195) is a major boss in the upper temple on the way to Emperor Ssraeshza.
  • The Emperor encounter begins with the Blood of Ssraeshza (NPC 162189, level 63, 200,000 HP, max hit 7,530). The Blood must be killed before the Emperor becomes targetable.
  • When the Blood is engaged, 4 corner trap NPCs spawn that cast Avatar Power, and 8 room guards spawn:
    • Grziz the Tormentor
    • Heriz the Malignant
    • Klazaz the Slayer
    • Nilasz the Devourer
    • Skzik the Tormentor
    • Slakiz the Malignant
    • Yasiz the Devourer
    • Zlakas the Slayer
  • Room guards respawn 120-150 seconds after being killed (random timer). They continue respawning until the Emperor is dead.
  • After the Blood dies, the Emperor "twitches" emote fires after ~10 seconds: "The Emperor twitches. He appears to have been weakened by the destruction of the golem." Then the real Emperor Ssraeshza (NPC 162491, level 66, 1,250,000 HP, max hit 50,000) spawns approximately 2 minutes later.
  • Curse of Ssraeshza timer: approximately 18.5 minutes after the traps enter combat, the emote "Something tells you that this room isn't safe anymore..." fires. Then approximately 5.2 minutes later, "A hissing echos in your ears..." and the Curse of Ssraeshza (spell 2069) is cast. Push DPS hard to avoid this.
  • Traps despawn either ~2 minutes or ~3.3 minutes after the Blood dies (random 50/50 chance between 115 and 200 seconds).
  • On Emperor death, 5 Wraith NPCs spawn in the chamber.
  • The High Priest of Ssraeshza (in the upper temple) has a reset mechanic: his adds are teleported back to their spawn point and healed if they are pulled to a lower floor (below z=240).
